Landscape Analysis

To emulate:

  • Information archeticture and menu structure the delineates tasks
  • Good use of video and/or large format imagery that captures the imagination of potential scholars
  • Clear presentation of data and success stories both for potential scholars and future partners

 

To stand out:

  • Wide spectrum of voices - high school through college, and beyond - can tell a story of broad and lasting impact
  • Partner message can be clearly separated from stakeholder message
  • Full Spanish translation

Learnings from Stakeholders

  • The need to visualize those elements of the Program, which are difficult to put into words. Some examples of this include:
  • The need for a “Scholar First” approach with an emphasis on the success of the scholars individually over the success of the organization as a whole.
  • The need to attract recent college graduates, who are job seekers to see the benefit of becoming part of the Schuler team.
  • The need for less text and more image and graphical representation of the Program descriptions.
  • The need for better organization of materials based on the type of person that is accessing the site

Entity Types

  • Scholar: content type to hold information on current and alumni scholars
  • Schuler Staff Member: content type to hold information related to Schuler staff members
  • Partner College: content type to hold information about partner colleges
  • Scholar Story - Text/Video: content type which holds text or video testimony of a Schuler Scholar’s personal story about their experience before, during, or after the Program with a focus on the transformative process.
  • Custom Page w/ layout: a content type for “evergreen” content, which can adapt itself to different layouts to serve the purpose of the particular page. Combined with the use of a reusable block system, this content will serve to make the site “on brand,” but also highly editable for a site admin.
